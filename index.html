UX 101

In ten minutes or less

The you're about to hackathon version

User eXperience

a person’s experience interacting with your (product/app/system/etc)

How a user feels

is it delightful? playful? serious? professional?

How a user can function

can they accomplish what they need to? can they tell what’s going on?

User-centered design

the notion that design decisions should be based on the needs and wants of users

Consider context

Attention

are they rushed (running to catch a bus) or are they expecting to sit down and read instructions (putting together furniture)?

Clarity

can they see everything they need clearly (or is a sign hiding behind a tree?)

Stress

are they coming to your site frustrated and looking for answers, trying to pay bills, looking to find a friend to communicate with?

Connectivity

are they sitting in a classroom and can easily get answers, or are they on a bus on their phone without internet?

Tap into motivation

We're driven by intrinsic things

  • autonomy
  • belonging
  • curiosity
  • love
  • learning
  • mastery
  • meaning

We're driven by extrinsic things

  • badges
  • competition
  • fear of failure
  • fear of punishment
  • gold stars
  • money
  • points
  • rewards

Know Your Scenarios

As a type of person

I want to do some thing

So that I can reap some benefit

As a student

I want to type my notes

So that I can easily find them later

As a visitor

I want to view train routes

So that I can get to my destination

As a television enthusiast

I want to be alerted when my favorite show comes on

So that I can tell my friends all the spoilers
